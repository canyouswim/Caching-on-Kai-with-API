3 steps to begin your adventure


1. Create an account

Create an account online or through this Geocaching® app to view a map of geocaches near you.


2. Find a geocache

Use the app to navigate to a geocache nearby. Don’t forget to bring a pen!


3. Share your experience

Once you find the geocache, sign and date the logbook. Place the geocache back where you found it and log your experience online.

Using the Compass View

This phone does not have physical compass built in. This means that to determine the correct bearing/direction to the select geocache, you must physically move around for the phone to determine which direction the cache is relative to your position.

The recommendation is to walk in a straight line for several feet until you see that the compass starts to move. From there you can correct the direction you are walking to start walking in the direction that the compass is pointing.
From the Map screen, you will see a small version of the compass that will also point you in the direction you need to travel, once the app has determined the correct bearing. The background will also change to green when the app is accurately determining your bearing, and change to yellow when there has not been enough movement to determine your bearing. When it is yellow, the compass is not likely pointing in the desired direction.

Nokia Phone Users

A special note if you use any of the Nokia phones (8110, 6300, 8000, probably 2720): the 4G antenna interfers with the A-GPS ability to obtain an accurate lock on your location. Use the "Phone Settings" button under the "Settings" screen to jump over to the phone's settings and turn off 4G when you require an accurate GPS lock.
